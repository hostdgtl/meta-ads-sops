# Remarketing

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Showing ads to people who have already interacted with your business — website visitors, email subscribers, past customers, social media engagers. Loomer built his reputation on remarketing strategies for a decade. He no longer recommends separate remarketing campaigns.

## Why It Matters

Audience Segments data proves that remarketing happens naturally with algorithmic targeting. Without any manual intervention, 10-40% of budget automatically goes to engaged audiences and existing customers. Separate remarketing campaigns are redundant and fragment budget.

## How It Works

1. Meta's Advantage+ Audience definition states that past conversions, pixel data, and prior engagement are prioritized by default
2. Without doing anything, Meta already favors people who know your brand
3. Audience Segments breakdown shows exactly how much budget goes to Existing Customers, Engaged Audience, and New Audience
4. The distribution adjusts dynamically based on performance, frequency, and cost signals
5. You get better optimization by letting Meta balance remarketing and prospecting than by manually splitting them

## Common Mistakes

- Running separate remarketing ad sets or campaigns (wastes budget, causes overlap)
- Not using Audience Segments to verify that remarketing is already happening
- Assuming broad targeting means Meta won't reach your warm audiences
- Over-investing in remarketing at the expense of prospecting new customers

## How to Apply It

- Define your Audience Segments in Advertising Settings (so you can see the breakdown)
- Run a Sales campaign with Advantage+ Audience and no manual remarketing setup
- Break down results by Audience Segments after 1-2 weeks
- If 10-40% of budget naturally goes to Engaged + Existing, your remarketing is handled
- Shut down separate remarketing campaigns and consolidate

## When to Make Exceptions

- Sequential messaging campaigns where the creative must follow a specific order
- Very high-value retargeting (cart abandonment email sequences are separate from ads)
- Extremely large budgets where you want granular control over remarketing frequency
