# Phase 1: Research & The Magic Brief

Build a fact-based creative foundation before touching a single asset.

---

## Objective

Produce a **Master Brief** containing 5-10 distinct creative hooks, grounded in real customer language, competitor insight, and audience psychology. No guesswork.

---

## Step 1 — Customer Language Mining

Use an LLM (Claude) to scrape and synthesise language from customer reviews, competitor comments, Reddit threads, and Q&A sections. The goal is to capture the **exact words customers use** to describe their problem and their ideal outcome.

### Prompt Template

```
You are a direct-response copywriter. I am going to give you [X] customer reviews for [product/brand].

Extract:
- The top 5 pain points
- The top 5 desired outcomes
- The top 5 emotional triggers

Return each as a short phrase in the customer's own words.
Then identify the single most common frustration across all reviews.
```

### Sources to Mine

- Product reviews on your own store
- Competitor product reviews (Amazon, Trustpilot, Google Reviews)
- Reddit threads in relevant subreddits
- Q&A sections on product pages
- Social media comments on competitor ads (Meta Ad Library)

---

## Step 2 — Competitor Benchmarking

Use the Meta Ad Library to identify competitors running ads. Use an LLM to categorise their creative formats and identify which emotional hooks they lean on most.

### Benchmarking Checklist

- [ ] Identify 3-5 competitor brands running Meta ads
- [ ] Screenshot 5+ ads per brand from the Meta Ad Library
- [ ] Classify format: UGC, Studio, Static, Carousel, Founder-to-camera
- [ ] Identify hook type: Problem/Solution, Social Proof, FOMO, Curiosity, Authority
- [ ] Note any gap — angles competitors are NOT taking

Use the [Competitor Benchmark Template](../templates/competitor-benchmark-template.md) to document findings.

---

## Step 3 — Build the Master Brief

Consolidate research into a structured brief. This brief is the input for [Phase 2 production](phase-2-modular-production.md).

### Brief Elements

| Element | Description / Example |
|---------|----------------------|
| Product / Campaign | The Handmade Soap Company — Gift Sets |
| Target Audience | Women 30-55, gift-buyers, natural beauty enthusiasts |
| Core Promise | Handcrafted in Ireland. Real ingredients. Real care. |
| Pain Points (verbatim) | "Sick of synthetic ingredients", "Want something that feels special" |
| Hook 1 | Problem/Solution — "You've tried everything. Your skin is still angry." |
| Hook 2 | Social Proof — "Over 10,000 Irish households switched." |
| Hook 3 | FOMO — "The collection sells out every Christmas. Don't wait." |
| Hook 4 | Founder Story — "Made by hand in a small workshop in Slane, Co. Meath." |
| Hook 5 | Curiosity — "Most soap has 12 synthetic ingredients. Ours has 4. All natural." |
| Winning Competitor Format | UGC unboxing with voiceover |
| Format Gap / Opportunity | No competitors using founder-to-camera in this category |

Use the [Master Brief Template](../templates/master-brief-template.md) to produce a clean, fillable brief.

---

## Deliverable

A completed **Master Brief** with:

- 5-10 hooks grounded in customer language
- Customer verbatim language (pain points, desires, triggers)
- Competitor format map with gap analysis
- Format recommendation for initial Ad Families
