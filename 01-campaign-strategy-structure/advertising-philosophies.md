# Advertising Philosophies

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

The foundational principles that guide modern Meta advertising strategy. Loomer's approach has shifted dramatically over 14+ years — from manual targeting and remarketing dominance to full algorithmic trust and creative-first strategy.

## Why It Matters

Your philosophy determines every tactical decision. Advertisers clinging to outdated approaches (complex structures, manual remarketing, audience micromanagement) are fighting a system designed to work differently now.

## How It Works

1. **Creative is the new targeting.** Your ads speak to specific people through pain points, personas, and angles — not through audience settings
2. **Trust the algorithm, verify with data.** Embrace Advantage+ Audience and use Audience Segments to confirm it's working
3. **Simplify structure, deepen creative.** Spend less time on campaign architecture and more on diverse, compelling ads
4. **"It depends" is the right answer.** Avoid one-size-fits-all rules. Context matters
5. **Don't blame the system for bad ads.** If results are poor, look at your creative and offer first

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming what worked 2-3 years ago still applies
- Treating Andromeda as something to "hack" or "please"
- Confusing correlation with causation when performance changes
- Refusing to let go of remarketing control despite evidence it happens naturally

## How to Apply It

- Audit your current strategy against these principles
- If running separate remarketing campaigns, check Audience Segments first
- Shift time investment from campaign structure to ad creative
- Test broad algorithmic targeting against your current approach

## When to Make Exceptions

- New ad accounts with zero pixel data may benefit from initial targeting signals
- Compliance requirements (special ad categories) override the broad approach
- Very niche B2B products with tiny addressable markets may need some targeting inputs
