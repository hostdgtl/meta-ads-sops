# Common Advertiser Mistakes

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

The most frequent strategic and tactical errors Meta advertisers make, based on Loomer's 14+ years of observation. These mistakes stem from outdated practices, misunderstandings about how the platform works, and resistance to algorithmic targeting.

## Why It Matters

Avoiding these mistakes is often more impactful than learning new tactics. Most underperforming accounts can be fixed by eliminating errors rather than adding complexity.

## How It Works

### Top Mistakes

1. **Overcomplicating campaign structure.** Multiple campaigns, many ad sets, granular audience splits — all fighting the algorithm and fragmenting budget

2. **Running separate remarketing campaigns.** Audience Segments prove remarketing happens naturally. Dedicated remarketing ad sets are redundant

3. **Restricting audiences too aggressively.** Hard age, gender, or placement exclusions shrink the optimization pool. Use Value Rules instead

4. **Not using Audience Segments.** Most advertisers don't define or check segments, leaving them blind to how budget distributes across customer types

5. **Blaming Andromeda for poor results.** Andromeda is a retrieval upgrade. If results dropped, examine your creative, offer, seasonality, and competition

6. **Creating "diverse" ads that look nearly identical.** Five photos with slightly different crops is not creative diversification. Ads must be meaningfully different

7. **Confusing correlation with causation.** Results changed after a platform update? That doesn't mean the update caused it

8. **Ignoring Advantage+ Creative enhancements.** Refusing AI-generated creative, text variations, or enhancements without testing them

9. **Not using Value Rules.** Accepting skewed delivery demographics when Value Rules could steer it without restricting audiences

10. **Making changes too quickly.** Not giving campaigns enough time to exit the learning phase before judging results

## Common Mistakes

- Reading this list and not auditing your own account against it
- Fixing one mistake while introducing another (e.g., consolidating campaigns but restricting audiences)

## How to Apply It

1. Audit your account against each item on this list
2. Pick the top 2-3 mistakes you're making and fix those first
3. Revisit monthly — as the platform evolves, new mistakes emerge
4. Share this list with team members managing campaigns

## When to Make Exceptions

- Some "mistakes" may be intentional for specific business reasons (e.g., hard age restrictions for compliance)
- New accounts with zero data may temporarily benefit from more structure
