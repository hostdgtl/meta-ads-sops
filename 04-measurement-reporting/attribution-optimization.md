# Attribution Optimization

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Attribution settings determine how Meta counts conversions — which clicks and views get credit for a conversion, and within what time window. The default is 7-day click, 1-day view. Your attribution setting also affects how Meta's algorithm learns and optimizes.

## Why It Matters

The attribution window you choose changes both your reported results AND how Meta optimizes delivery. A narrower window gives more conservative numbers but may starve the algorithm of learning signals. Understanding attribution prevents misreading performance.

## How It Works

1. **7-day click, 1-day view** (default): Counts conversions within 7 days of a click or 1 day of a view
2. **1-day click**: Most conservative — only counts conversions within 24 hours of a click
3. **Compare Attribution Settings**: A reporting tool that shows how results change across different windows
4. **First Conversion vs. All Conversions**: Choose whether to count only the first conversion per person or all conversions
5. Attribution optimization affects the learning phase — the algorithm learns from conversions within your chosen window

## Common Mistakes

- Never checking Compare Attribution Settings to understand how results shift across windows
- Using 1-day click attribution and wondering why results look low
- Not understanding that view-through conversions inflate numbers (someone saw your ad but may have bought anyway)
- Changing attribution settings mid-campaign without understanding the impact on optimization

## How to Apply It

1. Start with the default 7-day click, 1-day view
2. Use Compare Attribution Settings to see results across all windows
3. Cross-reference with your own analytics (Shopify, GA4) to find the most accurate window
4. If view-through conversions seem inflated, switch reporting to click-only and compare
5. Choose the attribution window that best matches your actual purchase cycle

## When to Make Exceptions

- High-consideration purchases (long sales cycles) may benefit from longer attribution windows
- Impulse purchases may only need 1-day click attribution
- If your analytics show most purchases happen within hours, 1-day click may be more accurate
