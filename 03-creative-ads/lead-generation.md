# Lead Generation

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-06-29 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Strategies for acquiring leads through Meta ads. This covers the choice between Sales and Leads objectives, Instant Forms, and quality optimization for lead campaigns.

## Why It Matters

Lead quality matters more than lead volume. A low cost-per-lead is meaningless if those leads never convert. Additionally, using the Sales objective when possible gives you access to Audience Segments — a critical reporting advantage.

## How It Works

1. **Sales vs. Leads objective:** Use Sales when possible (gives access to Audience Segments). Use Leads only when your conversion event is a form submission, not a purchase
2. **Instant Forms:** Meta's native lead forms that keep users on-platform. Fast submission but potentially lower quality
3. **AI Instant Form Generation (May 2026):** Meta can now auto-generate an Instant Form from a website URL or short text prompt. Rather than building forms manually through multiple screens, point the tool at your landing page URL and Meta drafts the form structure — questions, value props, and qualifiers. Useful for quick turnaround on straightforward campaigns (course signups, demo requests, basic e-commerce inquiries). Treat the output as a first draft: review and edit before publishing, especially for brand-sensitive language or complex qualification questions
4. **Embedded Appointment Booking (June 2026):** After a prospect submits an Instant Form, a supported scheduling partner's booking calendar can now load directly inside the Thank You page — with contact details carried over automatically. No re-entry required, no leaving the app. Setup: in the Instant Form editor, select the booking CTA and paste your scheduling link. The system auto-detects the calendar provider. Supported at launch: Calendly and HighLevel; HubSpot support coming in August 2026. Rollout is staged — check if your account has access yet. The goal is to capture appointments at the highest-intent moment immediately after form submission
5. **Quality optimization:** Test "Higher Intent" Instant Forms that add a review step before submission
6. **Tailored Leads campaigns:** An alternative setup that Meta provides for simplified lead campaign construction
7. **For Shopify D2C:** Leads often come through email signups, quiz funnels, free shipping offers, or content downloads

## Common Mistakes

- Optimizing only for cost-per-lead without measuring downstream conversion rates
- Using the Leads objective when Sales would work (losing Audience Segments access)
- Not following up on leads quickly enough (speed to contact matters)
- Making Instant Forms too short — some friction improves quality
- Publishing AI-generated Instant Forms without review — AI tends to sand off nuance, miss specific qualifiers, and default to generic value props

## How to Apply It

1. Determine if your goal is truly lead gen or if you can optimize for purchases (Sales objective)
2. If using Leads: set up Instant Forms with a review/confirmation step
3. For quick campaigns: try AI Instant Form Generation — enter your landing page URL, let Meta draft the structure, then edit for accuracy and brand voice before publishing
4. If using Calendly or HighLevel: enable Embedded Appointment Booking on the Instant Form Thank You page — check your account for access and paste your scheduling link in the booking CTA field
5. Test Higher Intent forms against standard forms — compare lead quality, not just volume
6. Connect forms to your CRM immediately for fast follow-up
7. Track lead-to-customer conversion rate, not just cost-per-lead

## When to Make Exceptions

- B2B with long sales cycles where purchase tracking isn't feasible — Leads objective is appropriate
- Event registrations, webinar signups — Leads objective with Instant Forms works well
- High-consideration purchases where the first conversion is always a lead
