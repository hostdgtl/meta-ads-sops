# Age and Gender Restrictions

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Hard limits on who can see your ads based on age range or gender. These exist in Audience Controls at the campaign or ad set level and restrict Meta's delivery to only the specified demographics.

## Why It Matters

Most advertisers restrict too aggressively based on assumptions about their audience. Hard restrictions reduce the audience pool Meta can optimize from, often hurting performance. Value Rules are almost always a better alternative.

## How It Works

1. Set in Audience Controls within the ad set or campaign settings
2. Age minimum/maximum and gender selection limit who is eligible to see your ad
3. These are hard cutoffs — Meta will not show your ad outside these bounds
4. Reduces the total addressable audience, which can increase costs and limit optimization

## Common Mistakes

- Restricting age to 25-45 because "that's our target market" when Meta could find buyers in other age groups
- Assuming demographic delivery skew means the algorithm is broken (it may be finding cheap conversions)
- Using restrictions when Value Rules would steer delivery without losing audience volume

## How to Apply It

- Default to no age/gender restrictions
- If delivery heavily skews to undesired demographics, try Value Rules first
- Only apply hard restrictions when legally required or for genuine brand safety

## When to Make Exceptions

- Legal requirements: alcohol (21+ in US), gambling, financial products
- Special ad categories: employment, housing, credit (Meta enforces restrictions automatically)
- Products that are genuinely single-gender (e.g., specific medical products)
