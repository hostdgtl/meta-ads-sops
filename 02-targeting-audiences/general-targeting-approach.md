# General Targeting Approach

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

The overarching strategy for who sees your ads. Loomer now fully embraces algorithmic targeting (Advantage+ Audience) over manual audience controls. Targeting happens through your creative, not your settings.

## Why It Matters

When optimizing for conversions, detailed targeting and lookalike audiences are automatically expanded — you can't avoid algorithmic targeting even if you try. Fighting it wastes effort and often produces worse results than embracing it.

## How It Works

1. Use Advantage+ Audience as the default — targeting inputs become "suggestions" not hard boundaries
2. Meta's system prioritizes past conversions, pixel data, and prior engagement automatically
3. Your ads do the targeting — speak to specific pain points, personas, and use cases
4. Audience Segments prove that remarketing happens naturally without separate campaigns
5. Andromeda's retrieval engine matches the right creative to the right person at scale

## Common Mistakes

- Obsessing over age, gender, or interest levers instead of focusing on creative
- Assuming Advantage+ Audience can't reach the right people
- Running separate ad sets for different audience segments
- Fighting algorithmic targeting when it's been working for years

## How to Apply It

- Set up Advantage+ Audience with no targeting inputs as your baseline
- Create ads that speak directly to your ideal customer's problems
- Use Audience Segments breakdown to verify who your ads are reaching
- If delivery skews to unwanted demographics, use Value Rules — not hard restrictions

## When to Make Exceptions

- Legal compliance requirements (special ad categories) may require restrictions
- Brand-new ad accounts with no pixel data may benefit from initial targeting signals
- Very niche B2B with tiny addressable markets may use targeting inputs as starting signals
