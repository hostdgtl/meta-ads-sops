# Audience Segments

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Audience Segments are a reporting feature (Sales objective only) that breaks down ad delivery into three groups: Existing Customers, Engaged Audience, and New Audience. Defined in Advertising Settings using custom audiences, they reveal how Meta distributes your budget across customer types.

## Why It Matters

This feature transformed Loomer's entire approach. Seeing that 20-30% of budget automatically went to remarketing audiences — with zero targeting inputs — gave him the confidence to abandon manual remarketing entirely. It's the single best tool for trusting algorithmic targeting.

## How It Works

### Defining Segments

1. Go to **Advertising Settings** in your ad account
2. Click the **Audience Segments** box

### Engaged Audience
- Use the broadest custom audiences possible
- All website visitors (180 days), entire email list
- Don't filter or pick "most valuable" — include everyone who has interacted
- Available custom audience types are limited (no Facebook Page, Instagram, or Video Views CAs)
- Engaged Audience numbers will always be understated because of these limitations

### Existing Customers
- Be precise — these are paying customers
- Use purchase event website custom audience (180 days) + email list of all buyers
- Sync with a third-party tool (e.g., Make/Zapier) to keep the list current
- Don't need separate CAs per product — one comprehensive list works

### Overlap Handling
- If someone is in both Engaged Audience and Existing Customers, Meta counts them as **Existing Customers** (lowest funnel wins)
- No need to manually exclude customers from Engaged Audience

### Using the Breakdown
- Ads Manager > **Breakdown** dropdown > Demographics > **Audience Segments**
- Shows rows for Existing Customers, Engaged Audience, New Audience
- Results only reflect impressions AFTER segments are defined — not retroactive
- Changes to definitions take effect from save time forward

### "Unknown" Results
- All results show as "Unknown" for non-Sales campaigns
- Small % of Unknown is normal even with proper setup
- High % may indicate a reporting bug or undefined segments
- Some results appear as Unknown temporarily before Meta categorizes them

## Common Mistakes

- Not defining Audience Segments at all (most advertisers don't use this feature)
- Being too narrow with Engaged Audience (filtering to "most engaged" instead of casting wide)
- Forgetting that definitions only apply going forward — not retroactive
- Trying to use Audience Segments with non-Sales objectives
- Confusing Audience Segments with targeting controls — they are reporting only

## How to Apply It

1. Define your Engaged Audience with the widest custom audiences available
2. Define your Existing Customers with all purchase-related custom audiences
3. Save and wait at least 1-2 weeks for data to accumulate
4. Break down your Sales campaigns by Audience Segments regularly
5. Use the distribution to validate that remarketing happens naturally
6. If the split looks wrong, check your segment definitions for completeness

## When to Make Exceptions

- Only available for Sales objective — if you need Leads or Awareness, you can't use this breakdown
- If your business has no repeat customers, Existing Customers segment will be small/empty (that's fine)
