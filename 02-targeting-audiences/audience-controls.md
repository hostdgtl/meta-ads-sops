# Audience Controls

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Campaign and ad set-level settings that restrict who can see your ads: location, age minimum, language, and other hard boundaries. These are distinct from Advantage+ Audience "suggestions" — controls are enforced limits.

## Why It Matters

Loomer rarely uses hard audience restrictions anymore. In most cases, Value Rules achieve the same delivery steering without reducing the audience pool. Hard controls should be reserved for compliance requirements, not performance optimization.

## How It Works

1. Set in the ad set or campaign targeting section
2. Location targeting: country, state, city, radius — always required
3. Age minimum/maximum: hard cutoffs on who sees your ad
4. Language: restrict to users with a specific language setting
5. Existing Customer Budget Cap: was available in ASC, now removed

## Common Mistakes

- Using audience controls as a performance lever instead of for compliance
- Restricting age/gender based on assumptions rather than data
- Not knowing that Value Rules exist as a softer alternative
- Over-restricting location when Meta could find buyers in adjacent markets

## How to Apply It

- Set location targeting to your serviceable markets
- Leave age and gender open unless legally required
- Use Value Rules instead of restrictions when delivery skews to unwanted demographics
- Review restrictions quarterly — remove any that aren't legally mandated

## When to Make Exceptions

- Legal compliance: alcohol (age), special ad categories (housing, employment, credit)
- Geographic: brick-and-mortar businesses with a fixed service area
- Language: if your ads are in English only and your market is multilingual
