# Budget Management

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

How to allocate and manage ad spend within a simplified campaign structure. With fewer campaigns and ad sets, budget management is about letting Meta distribute spend optimally rather than micromanaging allocation.

## Why It Matters

Fragmenting budget across too many ad sets causes auction overlap and starves the algorithm of data. A consolidated budget gives Meta's delivery system the flexibility to shift spend toward what's working.

## How It Works

1. **CBO is the default.** Campaign Budget Optimization distributes budget across ad sets automatically
2. **Meta adjusts dynamically.** Budget shifts between remarketing and prospecting based on performance signals
3. **Audience Segments show the distribution.** Break down results to see spend across Existing Customers, Engaged Audience, and New Audience
4. **Small budgets work.** $20-50 daily budgets can support 20+ ads if creative is diverse
5. **Existing Customer Budget Cap is gone.** Removed from ASC — segments are reporting only now

## Common Mistakes

- Spreading budget across too many competing ad sets
- Manually splitting budget between remarketing and prospecting ad sets
- Adjusting budget too frequently and resetting the learning phase
- Not checking Audience Segments to understand where budget actually goes

## How to Apply It

- Consolidate into fewer campaigns with CBO enabled
- Set budget at the campaign level, not ad set level
- Use Audience Segments breakdown weekly to monitor distribution
- Give campaigns enough budget to exit the learning phase (roughly 50 conversions per week)

## When to Make Exceptions

- Use ABO when running structured creative tests needing equal spend per ad set
- Geographic campaigns with different market sizes may need separate budgets
