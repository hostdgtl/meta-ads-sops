# Meta Andromeda

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Meta Andromeda is Meta's ad **retrieval** engine — the first step in ad delivery where the system narrows millions of ads down to a few thousand candidates that might be shown to a person. It is NOT an algorithm, and it doesn't rank the winning ads (that's GEM). Andromeda picks the shortlist.

## Why It Matters

Andromeda was built to handle the explosion of ad variations created by Advantage+ and generative AI. Where one ad used to be a single combination of text and image, it can now represent thousands of variations. The old retrieval system couldn't handle this scale. Andromeda processes hundreds to thousands of times more ads than its predecessor, enabling creative diversification to work at scale.

## How It Works

### What Andromeda Does
1. Takes a pool of tens of millions of ads
2. Narrows them to a few thousand candidates likely to be relevant to a specific person
3. Considers not just the right ad, but the right VERSION of an ad (text variation, image, enhancement)
4. Operates faster and with 10,000x more model complexity than the prior system
5. Runs in the background regardless of campaign type (manual or Advantage+)

### What Andromeda Is NOT
- **Not an algorithm** — it's one step (retrieval) in a multi-step delivery process
- **Not a targeting system** — it doesn't change what you should do with audience settings
- **Not the cause of bad results** — if anything, it improved delivery
- **Not something to "hack"** — there's no magic structure or ad count to "please" Andromeda

### Creative Diversification (The Key Lever)
Meta says creative diversification is the best lever for getting results with Andromeda. This means:

1. **Distinctly different visuals** — not minor tweaks, but meaningfully different images
2. **Different formats** — mix video, static images, carousels
3. **Flexible Format / Dynamic Creative** — up to 10 images/videos in a single ad
4. **AI-generated images and backgrounds** — quick diversification, can outperform originals
5. **Related Media** — up to 10 additional variations from past ads
6. **Advantage+ Creative enhancements** — overlays, animation, text improvements, etc.
7. **Primary text and headline variations** — up to 10 of each per ad

### Impact on Campaign Construction
- Simplified structure: one campaign per goal, fewer ad sets
- Targeting happens at the ad level through creative, not audience settings
- Use Advantage+ defaults for placements and delivery
- Old 6-ad-per-ad-set recommendation was removed — advertisers finding success with 10, 20, 50+ ads
- Works even on $20-50/day budgets with 20 ads

## Common Mistakes

- Treating Andromeda as an algorithm you need to "game"
- Blaming Andromeda for performance drops (confusing correlation with causation)
- Creating more ads without making them meaningfully different
- Ignoring creative diversification because "my one ad works fine"
- Following gurus who claim specific ad counts "please" Andromeda

## How to Apply It

1. Stop worrying about Andromeda specifically — focus on giving Meta diverse, quality creative
2. Build ads using all variation tools: text variations, multiple images/videos, Flexible Format
3. Enable Advantage+ Creative enhancements
4. Accept AI-generated creative and Related Media
5. Simplify your campaign structure (one campaign per goal, minimal ad sets)
6. Use Advantage+ Audience and let creative do the targeting
7. Monitor via Audience Segments, placement breakdowns, and creative breakdowns

## When to Make Exceptions

- If you have very limited creative resources, start small — even 2-3 meaningful variations help
- Brand-sensitive accounts should review AI-generated creative before accepting
- The core principle applies universally: more diverse creative = more options for the retrieval system
