# Placements

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-06-15 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Where your ads appear across Meta's network — Facebook Feed, Instagram Feed, Reels, Stories, Audience Network, Messenger, and more. Advantage+ Placements (automatic) is the default and recommended approach.

## Why It Matters

Different people consume content in different places. Restricting placements based on assumptions limits reach and increases costs. Instead of excluding placements, customize your creative to work well across all of them.

## How It Works

1. **Advantage+ Placements** (default): Meta distributes ads across all eligible placements based on performance
2. Meta optimizes delivery to placements where your ads are most likely to generate results
3. Different placements favor different formats — 9x16 for Reels/Stories, 1x1 or 4x5 for feeds
4. Creative diversification handles placement optimization — provide multiple formats and Meta matches them

### Notable Placement: Post-View Reels Ads (June 2026)

A new placement type that inserts ads between organic Reels clips — similar to mid-roll but positioned between separate Reels rather than inside one. Key mechanics:
- Only appears in Reels longer than 60 seconds
- Ads auto-play; viewers see a 5-second countdown before a manual skip button appears
- Skipping returns the viewer to the original Reel (not forward in the feed)
- Rolled out globally in June 2026; no manual opt-in required — available via Advantage+ Placements
- Supply 9x16 vertical creative to display correctly in this context

## Common Mistakes

- Excluding placements based on assumptions ("my audience isn't on Reels")
- Only creating 1x1 images and expecting them to work everywhere
- Not reviewing placement breakdowns to identify actual underperformers
- Manually selecting placements and missing opportunities

## How to Apply It

1. Use Advantage+ Placements as the default for all campaigns
2. Customize creative BY placement rather than restricting placements
3. Provide 9x16 video/images for Reels and Stories
4. Provide 1x1 or 4x5 for feed placements
5. Review placement breakdowns periodically
6. Only exclude a placement if data consistently shows it underperforms after sufficient spend

## When to Make Exceptions

- If a specific placement consistently shows very high cost per result with significant spend, consider excluding it
- Some creative formats (long-form video) may not suit certain placements
- Brand safety concerns may warrant excluding Audience Network
