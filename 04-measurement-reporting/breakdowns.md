# Breakdowns

> **Source:** Jon Loomer | **Last Updated:** 2026-04-28 | **Status:** Complete

---

## What It Is

Breakdowns let you slice your ad results by dimensions like demographics, delivery, action, and time. They reveal WHO your ads reach, WHERE they appear, and HOW different segments perform — without changing your campaign structure.

## Why It Matters

Breakdowns are your diagnostic tool. They replace the need for complex campaign structures (separate ad sets per audience) by giving you the same insights through reporting. The Audience Segments breakdown is particularly valuable — it shows how budget distributes across customer types.

## How It Works

1. In Ads Manager, use the **Breakdown** dropdown menu above your results table
2. Select a breakdown category: Demographics, Delivery, Action, or Time

### Essential Breakdowns
- **Audience Segments** (Demographics): Existing Customers, Engaged Audience, New Audience — the most valuable breakdown
- **Age** (Demographics): See which age groups your ads reach and convert
- **Gender** (Demographics): Distribution and performance by gender
- **Placement** (Delivery): Which placements (Feed, Reels, Stories, etc.) get spend and results
- **Platform** (Delivery): Facebook vs. Instagram vs. Audience Network performance
- **Device** (Delivery): Mobile vs. desktop breakdown

### Conversion Breakdowns
- Meta brought back breakdowns for conversion reporting after previously removing them
- You can now see conversion data sliced by these dimensions again
- Use Attribution breakdowns to see conversions by click vs. view attribution

## Common Mistakes

- Not using breakdowns at all and relying on top-line numbers only
- Using breakdowns to micro-optimize (pulling budget from every underperforming segment)
- Confusing breakdown insights with action items — some skew is normal
- Not understanding the Breakdown Effect (totals may not add up — see related SOP)

## How to Apply It

1. Check Audience Segments breakdown weekly for all Sales campaigns
2. Review placement breakdown monthly to identify consistent underperformers
3. Check age and gender breakdowns to see if delivery matches your ideal customer profile
4. If delivery skews heavily, consider Value Rules before making structural changes
5. Use breakdowns for directional insights, not precise accounting

## When to Make Exceptions

- Very new campaigns may not have enough data for meaningful breakdowns
- Some breakdowns are only available for specific objectives (Audience Segments requires Sales)
- Don't act on breakdowns with very small sample sizes
